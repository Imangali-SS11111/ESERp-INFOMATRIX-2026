# Explainable Social Engineering Risk Profiler (ESERP)

## Problem
People receive many messages in chats and social networks.
Some of them are not technical phishing attacks, but social engineering
attempts that manipulate emotions, money, fear or trust.

## Goal
To help non-technical users understand whether a message may be risky
and explain WHY it is risky in a human-readable way.

## What makes this project unique
- Focus on social engineering, not only phishing
- Explainable AI instead of black-box classification
- Educational and awareness-oriented approach
- Works offline and does not collect user data

## Risk Categories
1. Financial Manipulation
2. Psychological Pressure
3. phishing
3. Suspicious Commercial Activity
4. Safe / Neutral

## Example
Message: "We offer you money if you send this message"
Risk: Financial Manipulation
score:0.8
Explanation: Uses reward-based manipulation
conclusion:Potential threat using manipulation
Suggestions for answer;"Thank you,but i refuse this offer"
